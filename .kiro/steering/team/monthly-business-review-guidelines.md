# Team Steering: Monthly Business Review Guidelines

Use these guidelines when creating fictional Monthly Business Review materials from this repository.

All customers, revenue numbers, forecasts, and metrics in this repository are mock data for exercises. If a source file does not contain a number, create a clearly marked assumption rather than presenting it as fact.

## Required MBR Sections

Every MBR draft should include:

- Executive summary
- Account movement
- Revenue metrics
- Forecast impact
- Customer satisfaction signals
- Internal delivery KPIs
- Risks and blockers
- Decisions or asks
- Next-month actions

## Revenue Metrics

When available, include:

- Current ARR
- Current MRR
- Projected ARR
- Projected MRR
- Projected year-over-year growth
- Expansion opportunity
- Renewal or retention risk
- Revenue forecast impact

If exact values are missing, use a table with `Assumption` clearly marked.

Example:

| Customer | Current ARR | Current MRR | Projected YoY | Forecast impact | Confidence |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | --- | --- |
| Northstar Bakes | Assumption: GBP 36,000 | Assumption: GBP 3,000 | Assumption: +28% | Positive if analytics POC converts | Medium |

## Forecast Impact

Explain how each customer changes the revenue forecast.

Use these categories:

- `Positive`: likely to increase forecast
- `Neutral`: no meaningful change
- `Negative`: forecast at risk
- `Unknown`: not enough evidence yet

For each forecast impact, include the reason. Tie the reason to customer movement, blockers, adoption readiness, or stakeholder engagement.

## Customer Satisfaction Metrics

Include both quantitative and qualitative signals when available:

- Mock CSAT
- Mock NPS
- Support sentiment
- Executive sponsor sentiment
- Delivery confidence
- Product or platform friction
- Recent positive or negative feedback

Do not invent overly precise sentiment. Prefer simple labels such as `High`, `Medium`, `Low`, or `At risk` unless the task asks for numeric mock metrics.

## Internal KPIs

Track internal execution quality. Useful KPIs include:

- Follow-ups completed on time
- Open blockers
- Days since last customer touchpoint
- Discovery-to-proposal conversion
- Proof-of-concept readiness
- Architecture review completion
- Stakeholder report freshness
- Agentic workload candidates identified

Use internal KPIs to identify where the consultant or team needs to act, not to overstate customer commitment.

## AgentCore And Strategic Signals

When an account has an AgentCore candidate note, include:

- Candidate workload
- Business problem addressed
- Adoption readiness
- Data or integration prerequisites
- Human approval points
- Potential revenue expansion path

Do not position AgentCore as committed unless the customer notes explicitly say so.

## MBR Style

- Be concise and manager-friendly.
- Separate facts from assumptions.
- Prefer tables for account-level comparisons.
- Highlight deltas, blockers, and decisions.
- Use fictional but coherent numbers when the exercise requires financial metrics.
- Keep recommendations tied to next actions.

## Output Quality Bar

An MBR is complete when a manager can answer:

- Which accounts moved?
- Which accounts are stuck?
- What changed in revenue outlook?
- Which customers are happy, uncertain, or at risk?
- Which internal actions are overdue?
- What decisions are needed this month?
